Goods Imports & Exports with EU

UK goods trade with the EU has seen notable changes in recent years. Exports briefly dropped in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, and fell again as new Brexit trade rules took effect. While imports remained relatively stable, exports have struggled to recover, resulting in a sustained and widening trade deficit.

June 2016

The UK held a nationwide referendum in which a majority voted to leave the European Union.

March 2017

UK Prime Minister Theresa May triggers Article 50, the legal mechanism for a Member State to leave the EU.

October 2019

The UK and EU reached a revised withdrawal agreement. The new deal made changes to trade and customs arrangements, clearing the way for the UK's departure.

January 2020

After multiple extensions, the UK officially left the EU, ending its membership. A transition period began, during which EU rules continued to apply temporarily.

January 2021

The transition period ended and the EU-UK Trade and Cooperation Agreement provisionally came into effect, establishing a new framework for trade and cooperation between the two sides.
