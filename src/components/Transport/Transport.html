Transportation Modes for Goods Imports and Exports

Brexit has not only affected UK trade structure but also challenged the logistics of moving goods across borders.

Let's see how different transportation modes show the changes in trade networks and key transport hubs...

The UK's coastline is dotted with more than 120 commercial ports, which vary widely in size and specialisation. The southeastern ports primarily serve European routes, while western ports traditionally handle transatlantic trade.

Ports are essential to the UK economy, with around 95% of import and export tonnage being transported by sea.

Felixstowe Port

Felixstowe is the UK's largest container port, handling over 40% of all containerized trade.

After Brexit, the cargo transportation between the port and other regions showed a dynamic trend. Its cargo exports to the EU dropped significantly, while imports first increased and then decreased.

Dover Port

Dover is close to the mainland Europe and handles 17% of the UK's cargo trade, mainly serving cross-channel transport.

Brexit has had a significant impact on its cargo volumes and operations, with new customs procedures and documentation requirements.
